# Sales Script Creator

Bilingual (EN / DE) generator for a complete sales system: discovery script + closing framework + post-call scorecard + pattern-analysis template. Ground-truth sourced from YOUR customer transcripts, not invented.

## When to Run
- You're launching outbound into a niche you haven't sold into before.
- You have 4–10 customer interview or discovery transcripts ready.
- You need both a first-call discovery script AND a second-call closing framework, because you lose deals between the two.
- Triggered by: `/sales-script`, *"write me a sales script for [niche]"*, *"generate a discovery + closing pack"*.

## Why This Exists
Most sales scripts are written in the founder's language. They die on the first call because the prospect doesn't talk that way. This skill takes your customer's actual words — from transcripts — and puts them into a proven call structure. No invented pains. No invented numbers. No copy-paste templates that sound like every other pitch.

## Inputs You Must Provide
1. **Niche** — precise. Not "SMBs" — *"property managers with 500–2,000 units in German-speaking countries"*.
2. **ICP** — role (who signs), company size, segment, stage.
3. **Offer one-liner** — what you sell, outcome, price range.
4. **5–10 customer interview / discovery call transcripts** — the rawer and longer the better. Sales calls, validation interviews, support calls all qualify.
5. **Language** — `en`, `de`, or `both` for parallel output.
6. **Competitive set** — what alternatives the prospect considers (including "do nothing").
7. **Pricing flexibility** — what levers you can actually pull (discount, commitment length, payment terms, free months, guarantee).

If you don't have the transcripts, stop here and run them first. A script built on assumptions produces a script that fails on real calls.

## Output You Get

Two full scripts plus tooling, in the chosen language(s):

1. **Discovery Script** — 45–60 min call, Phases 1–5.
2. **Closing / Feedback Session Framework** — 30–45 min follow-up, Phases 1–6 including 5-Loop rescue.
3. **Post-Call Scorecard** — fill out after every call.
4. **Pattern Analysis Template** — run after 4–6 calls to learn and adjust.

## The Three Frameworks Underneath

### Straight Line (Belfort)
Every call has a straight line from opening to close. Deviations (objections, smalltalk) get heard, addressed, then led back onto the line. You must build three certainties:
1. **Product Certainty** — your offer solves the problem better than alternatives.
2. **You/Company Certainty** — you deliver personally and understand their world.
3. **Trust Threshold** — enough trust to move money.

### Value Equation (Hormozi)
`Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`
- Maximize Dream Outcome in THEIR words, not yours.
- Raise Likelihood with guarantees, personal onboarding, named proof points.
- Shrink Time Delay ("live next week").
- Shrink Effort ("you need nothing but X").

**Price rule:** the price does NOT come from you. The price comes from THEIR numbers. You let them quantify the cost of the problem. Your offer is a fraction.

### Million Dollar Weekend (Kagan) — Validation First
Every call is a data point. Even when no one buys, you win, because you know:
- How the daily workflow actually looks.
- Which pains are real and which you invented.
- What they will actually pay and why.
- What the true blocker is.

Run 4–6 calls → pattern analysis → sharpen offer → next 4 calls are 10x better.

## DISCOVERY SCRIPT — Phase-by-Phase Template

> Usage: replace every `[placeholder]` with the niche-specific content pulled from the provided transcripts. Keep the commands and structure untouched.

### PHASE 1 — Opening & Frame (3–5 min)

**Tone: warm, curious, peer-to-peer. Not salesy.**

**EN:**
> "Hey [First Name], glad this worked out. Before we start — how much time have you blocked for this?"
>
> "Cool. Here's how I'd love to run this: I want to first properly understand how your day looks — what works, what doesn't, what's costing you the most time and energy. I'll take 20–25 minutes for that. Then I'll tell you briefly what we do, and at the end we decide together whether this even fits. Sound good?"

**DE:**
> "Hey [Name], schön dass das geklappt hat. Bevor wir starten — wie viel Zeit hast du dir geblockt?"
>
> "Super. Ich sag dir mal offen, wie ich's mir vorstelle: Ich möchte erst mal richtig verstehen, wie dein Alltag aussieht — was gut läuft, wo's hakt, was dich am meisten Zeit und Nerven kostet. Dafür nehme ich mir 20–25 Minuten. Dann erzähle ich dir kurz, was wir machen, und am Ende entscheiden wir zusammen, ob das überhaupt zueinander passt. Passt das?"

**Trigger question:**
- EN: *"How did you come across us? What made you sign up?"*
- DE: *"Erzähl mal kurz — wie bist du auf uns gestoßen? Was hat dich dazu gebracht, dich einzutragen?"*

Write down the exact words. This is copy gold for the next campaign.

---

### PHASE 2 — Deep Discovery (20–30 min) — 60% of the call

**You talk at most 20% of the time.** The rest: listen, probe, take notes. *"The person who asks questions controls the conversation."*

#### 2A. Workflow — how they actually work
- **Niche-specific workflow opener** (pulled from transcripts): describe their Monday morning.
- Follow-ups: *"What comes next?"* / *"When in the day does it get too much?"* / *"What does a heavy week look like?"*
- Context: team size, tooling, volumes. **Ask for concrete numbers.**

#### 2B. Pain Mining — follow emotion, don't run a checklist

For each pain category found in the transcripts, probe:
- Quantify: *"How many per day/week?"*
- Time cost: *"How much time per week?"*
- Money cost: *"What does this cost you each month?"*
- Consequences: *"What happens when this goes wrong?"*
- Personal: *"Have you lost [customer / deal / person] because of this?"*

Stay with pain points when they become emotional. Go deeper, don't move on.

#### 2C. The Killer Questions — cost of nothing + competitive set

These are the core. They anchor price and produce the best copy material.

**EN:**
> "If you could fix one single thing in your workday right now — what would it be?" → their #1 pain. Write it down verbatim.
>
> "What have you tried to solve this so far?" → alternatives you actually compete with.
>
> "Why didn't it work?" → objections to pre-empt.
>
> "Have you looked at AI or automation for this?" → gauges sophistication and burn-scars.
>
> "What does not solving this cost you per month? Roughly?" → their own price anchor.

**DE:**
> "Wenn du eine einzige Sache in deinem Arbeitsalltag sofort ändern könntest — was wäre das?"
>
> "Was hast du bisher versucht, um das zu lösen?"
>
> "Und warum hat das nicht funktioniert?"
>
> "Hast du dich schon mal mit KI oder Automatisierung beschäftigt?"
>
> "Was kostet dich das pro Monat, dass du's nicht löst — ungefähr?"

---

### PHASE 3 — Value Anchor & Bridging (3–5 min)

Summarize what you heard. Use their words.

**EN:**
> "Just so I've got this right — the biggest things costing you time are [pain 1], [pain 2], [pain 3]. You've tried [alternative], and it broke because [reason]. You said it's worth roughly [€X / $X] a month to you. Did I miss anything?"

**DE:**
> "Nur damit ich's richtig mitgenommen habe — die größten Zeitfresser sind [Pain 1], [Pain 2], [Pain 3]. Ihr habt [Alternative] probiert, und das ist an [Grund] gescheitert. Du hast gesagt, es wäre euch ungefähr [EUR X] im Monat wert. Habe ich was übersehen?"

Wait for confirmation. Only now do you pitch.

---

### PHASE 4 — Pitch (5–10 min)

- One paragraph, 60–90 seconds spoken.
- Mirrors the customer's language from Phase 2C.
- One concrete proof point (named customer, specific before/after numbers) — from the transcripts.
- Ends with a clear next step, not a close.
- **Never pitch features without tying them to a pain they just named.**

---

### PHASE 5 — Soft Close & Next Step

**EN:** *"Honest question — does this sound like something worth trying in your world?"*
**DE:** *"Ehrliche Frage — klingt das nach etwas, das in deinem Alltag funktionieren würde?"*

- **Yes** → set the closing call. Send the offer 24h before. Send a 3-point summary of what you heard.
- **Maybe** → ask what's holding back; if valid, book the closing call anyway.
- **No** → Phase 6 of the closing framework (feedback collection — see below).

## CLOSING / FEEDBACK SESSION FRAMEWORK

**Context:** prospect had the discovery call, received the offer, had time to think. This call has ONE goal: a decision. Either close (design partner / first customer) or honest feedback on what the offer needs.

**Mindset:** you're not here to pitch again. You're here to collect a decision. Direct, flexible, honest.

### PHASE 1 — Setting the Scene (3–5 min)

**EN:**
> "Hey [Name], good to sit down again. Quick frame for today: we're here to make a decision — either we kick off onboarding together, or you give me honest feedback on what the offer still needs. Both are fine."

**DE:**
> "Hey [Name], schön dass wir uns wiedersehen. Kurz zum Rahmen: Wir treffen uns heute um eine Entscheidung zu treffen — entweder starten wir gemeinsam ins Onboarding, oder du gibst mir ehrliches Feedback, was das Angebot noch braucht. Beides ist völlig okay."

**Surface open points from both sides.** List them. Don't answer yet.

### Test Close (before clearing points)

**EN:** *"If we solve all these points today — when can we start?"*
**DE:** *"Wenn wir all diese Punkte heute zusammen lösen — wann können wir dann starten?"*

**Stay silent.** Their answer tells you where they stand:
- Concrete date → ready. Just clear the points.
- "We'll see" / vague → hidden objections. Phase 5 will surface them.
- New objection → capture and address in Phase 2.

### PHASE 2 — Clear the Points (5–10 min)
For each point: answer plainly → demo only if relevant → *"Is that cleared, or still sticking?"*

### PHASE 3 — Close (2 min)

**EN:** *"So — how have you decided?"*
**DE:** *"Also — wie habt ihr euch entschieden?"*

**Silence.** Let them speak first.
- **Yes** → Phase 4.
- **Hesitation / no** → Phase 5 (Looping).

### PHASE 4 — Onboarding (3 min)
Nail down: agreement sent today, onboarding start date, delivery window. Concrete date, not *"I'll be in touch."*

### PHASE 5 — Looping (10–15 min)

The offer is FLEXIBLE — but never free. Both sides commit something real.

**Levers you can pull:** reduce onboarding fee, free first months with commitment, monthly cancellation after commitment, money-back guarantee, structured feedback loops. **Never zero.** A prospect who pays nothing uses nothing seriously.

**Loop 1 — find the real objection:**
- EN: *"Okay, I hear it's not quite there. Help me understand — what's really holding you back?"* → then: *"If we fix [that], are you in?"*
- DE: *"Okay, ich höre, es passt noch nicht. Hilf mir verstehen — was hält euch wirklich zurück?"* → dann: *"Wenn wir [das] lösen — seid ihr dann dabei?"*

**Loop 2 — adjust the offer:**
- EN: *"What would the offer need to include for you to say yes today? Seriously — I'm flexible."*
- DE: *"Was müsste das Angebot beinhalten, damit ihr heute Ja sagt? Ernst gemeint — ich bin flexibel."*

**Loop 3 — design partner pitch:**
- EN: *"[Name], honest: we're early. I'm looking for partners who build this WITH me. That's why I can move significantly on price. Concretely: reduced onboarding, first [X] months free, [€Y] / month after that with a [Z]-month commitment. Only ask: [X] minutes of honest feedback per week. Your risk is [money-back guarantee]. What do you say?"*
- DE: *"[Name], ehrlich: Wir sind früh dran. Ich suche Partner, die [Produkt] MIT mir aufbauen. Deswegen kann ich beim Preis deutlich entgegenkommen. Konkret: reduziertes Onboarding, erste [X] Monate kostenlos, danach [EUR Y] im Monat, [Z]-Monats-Commitment. Einzige Bedingung: [X] Minuten ehrliches Feedback pro Woche. Dein Risiko ist [Geld-zurück-Garantie]. Was sagst du?"*

**Loop 4 — cost of waiting:**
- EN: *"On the last call you said [pain] costs you [€X] per month. It's been [Y] weeks since then. That's [€Z] burned. At what point does waiting stop making sense?"*
- DE: *"Beim letzten Call hast du gesagt, [Pain] kostet dich [EUR X] im Monat. Seitdem sind [Y] Wochen vergangen. Das sind [EUR Z] die du verbrannt hast. Ab wann lohnt sich Warten nicht mehr?"*

**Loop 5 — ask for honesty:**
- EN: *"[Name], an honest no is as valuable to me as a yes. If this isn't right — tell me, no problem. But if there's something I can change: what is it really?"*
- DE: *"[Name], ein ehrliches Nein ist für mich genauso wertvoll wie ein Ja. Wenn das nicht passt — sag mir das, kein Problem. Aber wenn es etwas gibt das ich ändern kann: was ist es wirklich?"*

### PHASE 6 — No Close → Collect Feedback (3 min)

If after looping still no. **Equally valuable.**

**Three mandatory questions — write answers verbatim:**

1. EN: *"Top 2–3 reasons you're not saying yes? Not the polite version — the real one."* / DE: *"Was sind die Top 2–3 Gründe warum ihr nicht Ja sagt? Nicht die höfliche Version — die echte."*
2. EN: *"What would the offer need to include for an instant yes?"* / DE: *"Was müsste das Angebot beinhalten, damit es ein sofortiges Ja wäre?"*
3. EN: *"Is there a price where you'd say yes immediately?"* / DE: *"Gibt es einen Preis bei dem ihr sofort Ja sagen würdet?"*

Close:
- EN: *"Thanks. That helps enormously. I'll check in again in [4–6 weeks] — and if anything changes in the meantime, drop me a line."*
- DE: *"Danke. Das hilft mir enorm. Ich melde mich in [4–6 Wochen] nochmal — bis dahin: wenn sich was ändert, schreib mir."*

## PATTERN ANALYSIS — run after 4–6 calls

1. **Which objection comes EVERY time?** → Address it in Phase 1 of discovery, not Phase 5 of closing.
2. **Which price was named most often?** → Test that as your new anchor.
3. **Which missing feature came up most?** → Roadmap priority.
4. **Close rate below 30%?** → The problem is in discovery or the offer, not the close.
5. **Which lever worked most often?** → Make it your standard.

## HOW THIS SKILL PROCESSES YOUR TRANSCRIPTS

When invoked, do this in order:

1. **Extract raw material from the transcripts:**
   - All verbatim pain statements → categorize (workflow, people, tools, cost, risk, emotional).
   - Quantified pain: any number the prospect said (hours, €, volume, frequency).
   - Failed alternatives: everything they've tried.
   - Language register: how they open emails, what they call their customers, their insider vocab.
   - Emotional moments: where voice drops, curses, laughs, pauses.

2. **Validate niche fit:** cross-check the claimed niche against what transcripts actually say. If the transcripts show a different ICP than claimed, flag it — don't paper over the mismatch.

3. **Populate the templates:** fill every `[placeholder]` in the Discovery and Closing scripts with specifics from the transcripts. Use verbatim language wherever you can.

4. **Produce in the requested language(s):** EN, DE, or both side-by-side. Never machine-translate between them — if both requested, write each in its native voice.

5. **Surface gaps:** at the end, list any template sections where the transcripts were too thin to populate (e.g. no data on cost of nothing). Flag them so the founder knows what to ask on the next call.

## Quality Bar
- A non-sales founder can run this script verbatim and get useful calls.
- Every specific claim (number, name, alternative) traces back to a transcript line.
- No invented pains, no invented numbers, no invented objections.
- Both languages read as natively written, not translated.

## Common Failure Modes
- Script feels like marketing copy → you over-generalised; re-run Phase 2 using more verbatim lines.
- Prospects aren't opening up → you're talking >20% of the time; cut your lines.
- Close rate low → the real blocker wasn't surfaced in discovery; run Phase 5 Loop 1 more aggressively.
- Using translated phrases in the non-native version → rewrite in that language's real operator voice, not translated.
