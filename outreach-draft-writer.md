# Outreach Draft Writer Skill

## When to Use
- "Write outreach messages for [prospect name/type]"
- "Draft LinkedIn DMs for [venture]'s prospects"
- "Create a connection request sequence for [prospect list]"
- "Personalize outreach using [founder]'s latest content"
- "Build an engagement-before-outreach plan for [prospect]"
- "Generate A/B variants for my outreach messages"
- "Help me warm up [prospect] before reaching out"

## Overview
Produce personalized, content-first outreach sequences — LinkedIn DMs, emails, and connection request notes — for any founder-led business. Every message leads with a relevant article, insight, or observation. Never a pitch.

The skill has 5 core capabilities:
1. **Prospect Intelligence** — Gather specific details about the prospect (company, recent posts, mutual connections, career milestones, life events) to fuel authentic personalization.
2. **Content Matching** — Map the founder's published content to the prospect's known interests, pains, or situation. Every outreach message anchors on a specific piece of content the founder has created.
3. **Engagement-Before-Outreach Playbook** — Orchestrate 1 week of genuine engagement (likes, comments, shares) on the prospect's content before any direct message. Warm the connection before opening it.
4. **Template-Driven Drafting** — Generate messages from prospect-type templates (exited founders, active owners, network connections, post-engagement) with Cialdini principles mapped to every element.
5. **A/B Variant Generation** — Produce 3-5 variants per message for systematic testing. Track which angles, hooks, and CTAs perform.

This skill is venture-agnostic. It works for any founder-led business where the founder's expertise and content are the primary trust signal. The Configuration Template below uses `{PLACEHOLDER}` syntax — fill in your own values.

## Core Frameworks (Always Apply)

### Robert Cialdini — 7 Principles of Influence (Mapped to Outreach)

Every outreach message must activate at least 3 of these 7 principles. The mapping below shows how each principle translates to a specific outreach tactic:

| Principle | Outreach Application | Example |
|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| **Reciprocity** | Lead with a gift — share a genuine insight, article, or observation BEFORE asking anything. The prospect should feel they received value. | "I wrote about {topic relevant to them} last week — thought of you because of your work on {X}. Here's the piece: {link}" |
| **Social Proof** | Reference patterns among peers — "several {peer-group} have told me...", "a common theme I'm seeing among {ICP descriptor}..." Never name-drop without permission. | "I've been having similar conversations with 3-4 {peer-group} in {their sector}" |
| **Authority** | Demonstrate expertise through specificity, not credentials. Reference specific numbers, named frameworks, precise analysis from the founder's content. Never say "I'm an expert." | "In our last analysis, we found {specific data point relevant to their pain — sourced and citable}" |
| **Liking** | Frame as peer, not vendor. Find genuine common ground — shared experiences, mutual connections, similar challenges. Mirror the prospect's communication style. | "Saw your post about {topic} — went through something similar when {founder's own analogous experience}" |
| **Scarcity** | Genuine capacity constraints only. Never fake urgency. Reference real limits: "We take on {N} new relationships per {period}", "I'm only sharing this analysis with a small group." | "We're quite selective about who we work with — capacity for maybe {N} more {clients/partners} this {period}" |
| **Commitment** | Micro-yeses that build momentum. Start with the smallest possible ask: a reply, a thought, a perspective. Each interaction slightly deepens the commitment. | "Would love to hear your perspective on this" → "Happy to share the full analysis" → "Open to a 15-min conversation?" |
| **Unity** | Same-tribe framing. "We" language. Shared identity: fellow founders, same peer network, same geography, same industry background. | "As someone who also {shared experience or background}..." / "Fellow {peer-network} member — saw your talk at {event}" |

### Dan Kennedy — Enter the Conversation Already in Their Head
- Research what the prospect is ALREADY thinking about (recent posts, company news, industry shifts)
- Open with THEIR reality, not yours
- The first sentence should make them think "how did they know I was dealing with this?"
- Never open with who you are or what you do

### Alex Hormozi — $100M Leads
- **Give value first:** The outreach message itself should be useful, not just a gateway to a call
- **Reduce perceived effort:** Make engaging easy — a reply, a thought, clicking a link. Not "book a 30-minute call"
- **Dream Outcome framing:** Hint at the outcome they want without promising it directly
- **"Free" as a strategy:** Share the thinking openly. The prospect realizes the founder's depth of insight is rare

### Russell Brunson — Attractive Character
- The founder's story IS the hook. Their origin, their contrarian beliefs, their unique experience
- **Epiphany Bridge in miniature:** Even a 300-character DM can contain a micro-story that leads to an insight
- **Identity play:** The prospect should see the founder as "someone like me, but with an answer I don't have yet"
- **Vulnerability > perfection:** A message that shares a mistake or a learning moment outperforms polished credentials

## Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)
- **Never pitch the product or service directly.** Every message leads with content, insight, or genuine observation.
- **Always reference specific content the founder has published.** Link to an article, quote a post, mention a video. The content IS the value delivery.
- **Match language to the prospect.** Sie/Du for German. Formal/casual for English. Mirror their LinkedIn tone.
- **Connection requests: under 300 characters.** LinkedIn truncates at ~300. Make every character count.
- **Messages (DMs and emails): under 500 characters.** Respect their time. Dense value, no filler.
- **Soft CTA only.** "Would love your perspective", "happy to share more", "curious what you think." Never: "book a call", "schedule a demo", "let's set up a meeting."
- **No unsolicited attachments or links in first touch.** Earn the right to share content.
- **Personalization must be genuine.** If you can't find something specific about the prospect, don't fake it. Generic personalization ("I see you're a leader in your field") is worse than none.
- **One message, one idea.** Never stack multiple asks or multiple topics into a single message.

## Prospect Type Templates

### Template 1: Exited Founders
**Profile:** Sold their business in the last 0-5 years. Sitting on liquidity. Overwhelmed by wealth management options. Competent in business, uncertain in investing.

**Cialdini mapping:** Unity (fellow founder tribe) + Reciprocity (free insight) + Authority (specific expertise)

**Connection request (< 300 chars):**
```
{Prospect first name}, congrats on the {company/exit context}. I recently wrote about what founders typically face post-exit with their liquidity — thought it might resonate. Would be great to connect.
```

**Value message — Day 3 (< 500 chars):**
```
Thanks for connecting, {first name}. As promised — I wrote this piece after conversations with several {peer-group}: {link to article}. The most common pattern I see: {one-line pattern from your real customer transcripts that the prospect will recognise}. Curious if that matches your experience.
```

**Follow-up — Day 7 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, one more thing from that piece — {specific data point from the article, sourced and citable} tends to surprise people. Happy to share the source data if it's useful for your own work.
```

**Soft close — Day 14 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, I've been enjoying your posts on {topic from their feed}. If you ever want to compare notes on {relevant topic that connects their world to your offer}, happy to share our framework. No agenda — just find it's a conversation worth having with people who think about this seriously.
```

### Template 2: Active Business Owners
**Profile:** Still running their business. Wealth concentrated in the company. Thinking about liquidity events, diversification, or succession. Time-poor, trust-scarce.

**Cialdini mapping:** Liking (peer respect) + Authority (business-specific insight) + Commitment (micro-yes)

**Connection request (< 300 chars):**
```
{First name}, been following {company name}'s growth — impressive trajectory in {sector}. I write about wealth strategy for owner-operators. Thought there might be overlap. Would be glad to connect.
```

**Value message — Day 3 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, thanks for the connection. I published a piece last month that came directly from a conversation with a founder in a similar position to yours — {link}. The core question: when you've built significant value inside one company, how do you think about wealth outside of it? No rush — just thought it might be relevant given where {company name} is.
```

**Follow-up — Day 7 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, quick follow-up — one insight from that article that owner-operators consistently react to: {specific insight, e.g., "the difference between managing a portfolio and owning businesses through a portfolio"}. It's a framing shift more than a strategy shift. Curious whether that distinction lands for you.
```

**Soft close — Day 14 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, saw your post about {topic}. Made me think of a framework we use for exactly that scenario. Happy to share it — no strings, just find it's the kind of thing that's more useful in a conversation than a PDF. Let me know if you'd be up for a quick exchange sometime.
```

### Template 3: Network Connections (EO, YPO, Peer Groups)
**Profile:** Member of the same founder/entrepreneur network. Shared identity is the strongest bond. These prospects expect peer-level communication, not sales outreach.

**Cialdini mapping:** Unity (same tribe) + Liking (shared experience) + Reciprocity (give first)

**Connection request (< 300 chars):**
```
{First name}, fellow {EO/YPO/network} member here. Saw your {talk/post/profile} — your point about {specific observation} stuck with me. Would be great to be connected.
```

**Value message — Day 3 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, great to connect. I've been writing about {topic relevant to network audience} — this piece came out of a conversation at {event/forum name}: {link}. A few {EO/YPO} members have told me it reframed how they think about {topic}. Would value your perspective — you've clearly thought deeply about this.
```

**Follow-up — Day 7 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, following up on that article — the part about {specific section} got the most responses. The common thread: {insight from peers}. I'm considering writing a follow-up focused on {next angle}. If that's a topic you care about, I'd love to include your perspective (anonymized, of course).
```

**Soft close — Day 14 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, I'm hosting a small {virtual roundtable / dinner / conversation} for {network} members on {topic} — {date, if applicable}. Very informal, 5-6 people, no presentations. Just a structured conversation. Would you be interested? Happy to share the invite if so.
```

### Template 4: Post-Content-Engagement
**Profile:** Someone who engaged with the founder's content — liked, commented, shared, or replied. The warmest lead. They've already signaled interest.

**Cialdini mapping:** Commitment (they already engaged) + Reciprocity (acknowledge + give more) + Social Proof (others found it valuable too)

**Connection request (< 300 chars):**
```
{First name}, thanks for the thoughtful {comment/reaction} on my {post/article about topic}. Your point about {their specific comment} added something I hadn't considered. Would love to connect and continue the conversation.
```

**Value message — Day 3 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, your {comment} on {topic} sparked a deeper look on my end. I pulled together {additional data/follow-up analysis/related piece} that builds on exactly the point you raised: {link or insight}. Thought you'd want the extended version since you're clearly thinking about this.
```

**Follow-up — Day 7 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, a few other people who engaged with that piece asked similar questions — I'm working on a follow-up that goes deeper into {specific angle}. If there's a particular aspect you'd want me to address, I'd genuinely welcome the input. Better to write what people actually need than what I assume.
```

**Soft close — Day 14 (< 500 chars):**
```
{First name}, the follow-up piece is live: {link}. I credited the conversation thread that sparked it (no names). If this topic is something you think about regularly, I'd enjoy a direct exchange — I find the best insights come from conversations, not monologues. Happy to share more anytime.
```

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Gather Prospect Intelligence
Before writing a single word, collect or confirm the following for each prospect:

**Required (minimum viable personalization):**
- [ ] **Full name** and correct title/salutation
- [ ] **Company name** and what it does (1 sentence)
- [ ] **Prospect type:** exited founder / active owner / network connection / post-engagement
- [ ] **Language & formality:** English formal, English casual, German Sie, German Du
- [ ] **One specific detail:** a recent post, a company milestone, a talk they gave, an article they wrote, a shared connection

**Ideal (for high-priority prospects):**
- [ ] **Career trajectory:** where they've been, what they built, what they exited
- [ ] **Recent content:** their last 5-10 LinkedIn posts (topics, tone, engagement level)
- [ ] **Company news:** funding rounds, product launches, hires, expansions, exits
- [ ] **Mutual connections:** shared contacts, shared networks, shared events
- [ ] **Life events:** awards, board appointments, speaking engagements, relocations
- [ ] **Pain signals:** anything they've posted about challenges, frustrations, decisions they're weighing

**Sources to check:**
- LinkedIn profile + recent activity
- Company website / About page
- Google News (company name + founder name)
- Crunchbase / PitchBook (if applicable)
- Network directories (EO, YPO, local chamber)
- Their own content (articles, podcasts, videos)

### Step 2: Match Founder Content to Prospect
Map the founder's published content library to the prospect's known interests:

1. **List the founder's recent content** (last 10-20 pieces: articles, LinkedIn posts, videos, newsletter editions)
2. **For each piece, tag the prospect relevance:** Which prospects would find this specific piece valuable? Why?
3. **Select the strongest match** — the one piece that most directly speaks to something the prospect is dealing with or has expressed interest in
4. **Identify the anchor insight** — the specific data point, framework, or observation from that content piece that will be quoted or referenced in the message

**Content-to-prospect matching rules:**
- The match must be genuine — if no content fits, do not force it. Write a custom insight instead.
- Prefer recent content (last 30 days) over older pieces
- Prefer content that addresses the prospect's known pain over content that showcases the founder's expertise
- The prospect should feel "this was written for someone exactly like me" — not "they sent this to everyone"

### Step 3: Engagement-Before-Outreach (1 Week Pre-Sequence)
Before sending any direct message, execute 7 days of genuine engagement on the prospect's content:

| Day | Action | Purpose |
|-----|--------|---------|
| Day 1 | Like 2-3 of their recent posts | Get your name in their notifications |
| Day 2 | Leave a thoughtful comment on their most recent post (2-3 sentences, add a genuine perspective or question) | Show you actually read their content |
| Day 3 | Share one of their posts with a brief commentary to your network | Cialdini: Reciprocity — give visibility before asking for attention |
| Day 4 | Like another post + reply to someone else's comment on their post with an additive insight | Build ambient familiarity |
| Day 5 | No action (avoid being overbearing) | Natural rhythm |
| Day 6 | Comment on another post — this time, reference one of the founder's own published pieces if it's relevant to the discussion | Bridge the founder's content into the prospect's world |
| Day 7 | Send the connection request | By now, your name is familiar. The request feels natural, not cold. |

**Rules for engagement-before-outreach:**
- All engagement MUST be genuine. If you can't add a real thought, don't comment — just like.
- Never use generic comments ("Great post!", "Thanks for sharing!", "Insightful!"). These signal automation.
- Comments should be 2-4 sentences: acknowledge their point, add a perspective or question, optionally reference a data point.
- Do NOT mention your product, service, or company during the engagement phase. This is about being a thoughtful peer, not warming up a sales lead.
- If the prospect engages back (replies to your comment, likes your comment), acknowledge it naturally. Conversation > broadcast.

### Step 4: Write Outreach Variants
For each prospect, generate messages for the full sequence using the appropriate Prospect Type Template:

1. **Select the template** based on prospect type (exited founder, active owner, network, post-engagement)
2. **Personalize every variable:** prospect name, company context, content link, specific detail, relevant insight
3. **Generate 3-5 variants for each message** in the sequence (A/B testing)
4. **Tag each variant** with the primary Cialdini principle it leads with

**Variant generation rules:**
- Variants should differ in **angle**, not just wording. Different hooks, different content references, different personalization anchors.
- Variant A: Lead with content (Reciprocity)
- Variant B: Lead with specific observation about the prospect (Liking)
- Variant C: Lead with shared identity/tribe (Unity)
- Variant D: Lead with a surprising data point (Authority)
- Variant E: Lead with a question that names their pain (Dan Kennedy — enter the conversation)

**Output format for each variant:**
```markdown
### {Prospect Name} — {Sequence Step} — Variant {A/B/C/D/E}
**Cialdini lead:** {principle}
**Content anchor:** {which article/post is referenced}
**Personalization anchor:** {what specific detail about the prospect}
**Character count:** {number}/300 or /500

{Message text}
```

### Step 5: Assemble the Sequence & Timing
Organize the full outreach sequence with timing:

```
Week 0 (Engagement Phase):
  Day 1-7: Engagement-before-outreach playbook (Step 3)

Week 1 (Connection):
  Day 8: Send connection request (< 300 chars)
  — Wait for acceptance —

Week 1-2 (Value):
  Day 3 after acceptance: Value message with content link (< 500 chars)

Week 2 (Follow-up):
  Day 7 after acceptance: Follow-up referencing specific insight (< 500 chars)

Week 3 (Soft Close):
  Day 14 after acceptance: Soft CTA — perspective exchange, roundtable invite, or direct conversation offer (< 500 chars)
```

**Timing rules:**
- Never send more than 1 message per week after connection
- If the prospect replies at any point, STOP the sequence and have a real conversation
- If no reply after the soft close, wait 30 days. Then re-enter with fresh content (new article, new insight). Do NOT repeat the same sequence.
- Best send times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am in the prospect's local timezone
- Never send on weekends or holidays

### Step 6: Review & Deliver

**Delivery format:**
For each prospect, produce a single document containing:
1. Prospect intelligence summary (Step 1 output)
2. Content match rationale (Step 2 output)
3. Engagement plan (Step 3 — 7-day calendar)
4. All sequence messages with variants (Step 4 output)
5. Sequence timeline (Step 5 output)

Save to: `{venture-folder}/outreach/{YYYY-MM}-{prospect-slug}.md`

If running for a prospect batch, also produce a tracker:
```markdown
| Prospect | Type | Content Match | Engagement Start | Connect Sent | Status |
|----------|------|---------------|-----------------|--------------|--------|
| {name} | {type} | {article title} | {date} | {date} | {stage} |
```
Save tracker to: `{venture-folder}/outreach/tracker-{YYYY-MM}.md`

## Quality Checklist
Before delivering any outreach sequence, verify:
- [ ] **No pitch** — every message leads with content, insight, or observation, never a product/service description
- [ ] **Content referenced** — each message anchors on a specific piece of the founder's published content
- [ ] **Genuine personalization** — at least one detail specific to this prospect that could not apply to anyone else
- [ ] **Character limits respected** — connection requests < 300 chars, messages < 500 chars
- [ ] **Cialdini principles mapped** — at least 3 of 7 principles active across the sequence, each message tagged
- [ ] **Soft CTA only** — no "book a call", "schedule a meeting", "let's hop on a quick chat"
- [ ] **Language matched** — Sie/Du correct for German, formality level matches the prospect's own communication style
- [ ] **Engagement-before-outreach planned** — 7 days of genuine engagement before any DM
- [ ] **3-5 variants per message** — different angles, not just rewording
- [ ] **Timing rules followed** — max 1 message/week post-connection, best send times, no weekends
- [ ] **Stop triggers defined** — if prospect replies, sequence stops; if no reply after soft close, 30-day pause
- [ ] **Founder voice** — messages sound like the founder, not like a marketing agency or AI tool
- [ ] **No generic filler** — every sentence adds value or advances the relationship
- [ ] **Unity framing** — "we" and peer language, not vendor-to-prospect language

### Sequence Timeline (generic — adapt cadence to your market)

```
PRE-SEQUENCE (Week 0):
  Day 1: Like 2-3 of prospect's recent LinkedIn posts
  Day 2: Comment on their most recent post (2-3 sentences, genuine insight)
  Day 3: Share one of their posts with brief commentary
  Day 4: Like + reply to a commenter on their post
  Day 5: Rest
  Day 6: Comment on another post — bridge to one of your articles if natural
  Day 7: Send connection request (pick template by prospect type)

POST-CONNECTION (Weeks 1-3):
  Day 3 after acceptance:  Value message — link to matched article
  Day 7 after acceptance:  Follow-up — specific data point or framework
  Day 14 after acceptance: Soft close — exchange, roundtable, or dinner invite

  Wait 30 days → Re-enter with new content piece → New engagement cycle

IF PROSPECT REPLIES AT ANY POINT:
  STOP sequence → Have a real conversation → Founder takes over directly
```

## Anti-patterns
- **Sending the connection request without 5 days of pre-engagement.** Request-rejection rate jumps. Pre-engagement (likes + comments + shares) earns the right to message.
- **Identical opener across all 4 prospect types.** The templates exist precisely because Trigger / Operator / Peer / Post-Engagement need different hooks. Same opener everywhere = no opener.
- **Pitch in the value message.** Keep Day-3 pure value. The close is Day-14. Pitching too early collapses the sequence.
- **Using `{first_name}` without checking it's actually their first name.** "Dr.", "Prof.", or a surname in that field reads as a bot in 0.1 seconds.
- **Linking content longer than 5 minutes to read.** They won't read it. They'll dodge. Lead with a 90-second piece or a single insight; save the long read for follow-up.
- **Skipping the pre-sequence warm-up because it feels slow.** It is slow. It also doubles reply rates. The patient version compounds; the impatient version flatlines.

## Adaptation Notes
This skill is venture-agnostic. When applying:
- Fill the Configuration Template above using your Asset Foundations.
- Adjust the four prospect types to match your ICP — a SaaS founder might have templates for CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and agency owners; a services founder might have templates for buyers vs. influencers vs. referral partners.
- Adapt the content matching to whatever your founder publishes — blog posts, YouTube videos, podcast episodes, Twitter threads, case studies.
- For B2B SaaS: lead with ROI data and use-case relevance instead of peer identity.
- For services: lead with transformation stories and trust signals.
- For consumer brands: lead with community and identity alignment.
- For German-market ventures: default to Sie unless the prospect uses Du on their own LinkedIn.
- Adjust sequence timing for market norms — DACH audiences may prefer slower cadence (2 weeks between messages); US/UK audiences tolerate weekly.
