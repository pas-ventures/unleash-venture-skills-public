# Content Engine Skill

## When to use
- "Create content pieces for [venture/brand]"
- "Turn this [partner question / analysis / market event] into an article"
- "Build a content calendar for [venture]"
- "Write headlines and drafts for [topic]"
- "Help me produce 10 content pieces this month"
- "Analyze my target group for content creation"
- "Learn my voice from my existing LinkedIn/YouTube/Substack content"
- "Create a Google Doc / Notion page with the draft so I can review it"
- "Optimize this piece for [LinkedIn / Substack / Instagram / TikTok]"

## Overview
Transform raw inputs — partner questions, investment analyses, market events, contrarian observations, call transcripts — into publish-ready content pieces (articles, LinkedIn posts, video scripts, newsletter editions) that position the founder as the category authority.

The skill has 4 core capabilities:
1. **Voice Training** — Ingest the founder's existing content (LinkedIn, YouTube, Substack, newsletters, internal writing) to learn their authentic tone, vocabulary, and style. The skill writes AS the founder, not FOR them.
2. **Target Group Research** — Deep-scrape YouTube transcripts, forums, Reddit, industry reports, and competitor content to build a comprehensive "Asset Foundations" document of the target audience's real pains, language, and failed alternatives.
3. **Framework-Driven Content Production** — Every piece applies Dan Kennedy (PAS), Alex Hormozi ($100M Offers/Leads), Russell Brunson (Expert Secrets), and Robert Cialdini (7 Principles) to maximize engagement and trust.
4. **Platform-Optimized Delivery** — Format content for each platform's best practices (Substack, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, Medium, website) and deliver via Google Docs, Notion, or markdown for iterative founder review.

This skill is venture-agnostic. It works for any founder-led business where the founder IS the brand. The Configuration Template below uses `{PLACEHOLDER}` syntax — fill in your own values.

## Core Frameworks (Always Apply)

### Dan Kennedy — Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)
- **Problem:** Name the reader's pain in their own words — enter the conversation already in their head
- **Agitation:** Make the cost of the problem vivid, specific, and urgent (EUR amounts, years lost, compounding damage)
- **Solution:** Present the founder's perspective as the inevitable answer — not a pitch, but a worldview shift
- **"One to Many" Sales Letter:** Every article is a sales letter disguised as insight. Lead with the reader's problem, not your credentials.

### Alex Hormozi — $100M Offers + $100M Leads
- **Value Equation:** Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood / Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice — every content piece should increase perceived likelihood of the dream outcome while reducing perceived effort
- **Grand Slam Offer (implicit):** Content should make the reader think "if this is what they give away for free, what do they charge for?"
- **Lead Magnets as Content:** Every article should be valuable enough to be a standalone lead magnet
- **100M Leads "Give Away the Secrets, Sell the Implementation":** Teach the framework openly. The reader realizes they need the founder to execute it.

### Russell Brunson — Expert Secrets + DotCom Secrets
- **Attractive Character:** The founder IS the content. Their origin story, beliefs, contrarian positions, and vulnerability create connection.
- **Epiphany Bridge:** Every article tells a story that leads to a breakthrough realization. Not "here's what to do" but "here's the moment I understood WHY."
- **False Beliefs:** Identify and systematically dismantle the 3 false beliefs the audience holds:
  1. **Vehicle:** "This approach/solution won't work" → Prove the model works
  2. **Internal:** "I can't do this / I'm not sophisticated enough" → Show it's accessible
  3. **External:** "External forces prevent this" → Remove perceived barriers
- **Hook-Story-Offer:** Every piece hooks attention (headline), tells a story (body), and makes an implicit offer (CTA)

### Robert Cialdini — 7 Principles of Influence
Apply these throughout all content:
1. **Reciprocity:** Give genuine value first. The reader should feel indebted before any ask.
2. **Social Proof:** Reference conversations with peers, anonymized client situations, industry data showing others making the same choice.
3. **Authority:** Demonstrate expertise through specificity — exact numbers, named frameworks, precise analysis. Not "I'm an expert" but showing it through depth.
4. **Liking:** Write as a peer, not a vendor. Share vulnerabilities, acknowledge complexity, use humor where appropriate.
5. **Scarcity:** Natural scarcity only — limited capacity, invitation-only events, exclusive access. Never fake urgency.
6. **Commitment/Consistency:** Each piece should invite a micro-commitment (subscribe, share, reply) that moves the reader toward the next step.
7. **Unity:** "We" framing — founders like us, entrepreneurs who think this way, people who refuse to accept the status quo.

## Content Types & Templates

### Type 1: Partner Question Article (40% of output)
**Source:** A real question from a client, partner, prospect, or peer.
**Format:**
```
Headline: [Specific question or provocative reframe]

"A [partner/founder/client] asked me recently: [exact question]"

[Why this question matters — PAS: name the pain behind the question]

[Your analysis — be specific, use numbers, name names where appropriate]

[The counterintuitive insight — Epiphany Bridge moment]

[What this means for the reader — connect to their situation]

[Implicit CTA — "If you're thinking about this too, [soft invitation]"]
```
**Length:** 500-800 words
**Frameworks:** DK (PAS), Brunson (Epiphany Bridge), Cialdini (Authority + Reciprocity)

### Type 2: Investment/Market Analysis (20% of output)
**Source:** An analysis the founder has already done, a thesis, a research piece.
**Format:**
```
Headline: [Contrarian claim or surprising conclusion]

[The conventional wisdom — what most people believe]

[Why it's wrong — specific data, named sources, concrete examples]

[The real framework — how the founder thinks about this differently]

[What this means for wealth/business/the reader — make it actionable]

[One clear takeaway the reader can apply immediately]
```
**Length:** 800-1,200 words
**Frameworks:** DK (Discredit alternatives), Hormozi (Give away secrets), Cialdini (Authority + Social Proof)

### Type 3: Contrarian/Challenger Piece (15% of output)
**Source:** Industry practices the founder disagrees with. Conventional wisdom that's wrong.
**Format:**
```
Headline: [Bold, potentially uncomfortable claim]

[The lie/myth/industry practice — stated clearly]

[Why it persists — who benefits from the status quo]

[The real numbers — what it actually costs the reader (DK: Agitate)]

[The alternative — what smart operators do instead]

[Why the founder built their business differently because of this]
```
**Length:** 600-1,000 words
**Frameworks:** Brunson (False Beliefs), DK (Agitate + Discredit), Cialdini (Unity — "we see through this"), Hormozi (Opportunity Cost)

### Type 4: Market Commentary / "Update" (10% of output)
**Source:** A market event, economic shift, regulatory change, or breaking news relevant to the audience.
**Format:**
```
Headline: [Event] + [What it means for you]

[What happened — 2-3 sentences, factual]

[Why most people are reacting wrong]

[What the founder is actually doing / thinking about this]

[What the reader should consider — not financial advice, but a thinking framework]
```
**Length:** 400-600 words
**Frameworks:** Cialdini (Authority — calm expertise amid chaos), Hormozi (Reduce time delay — give the answer fast), DK (Enter the conversation)

### Type 5: Video/Podcast Script (10% of output)
**Source:** Any of the above topics, reformatted for spoken delivery.
**Format:**
```
[Hook — first 5 seconds must create curiosity or tension]

"Here's the thing nobody tells you about [topic]..."

[Story or example — personal, specific, vivid]

[The insight — the Epiphany Bridge moment]

[Call to engage — "What do you think? Drop a comment / DM me your take"]
```
**Length:** 3-5 minutes spoken (500-750 words)
**Frameworks:** Brunson (Hook-Story-Offer, Attractive Character), Cialdini (Liking — be human on camera)

### Type 6: LinkedIn Post / Short-Form (5% of output)
**Source:** One insight extracted from a longer piece, or a standalone observation.
**Format:**
```
[One-line hook — pattern interrupt]

[3-5 lines expanding the insight]

[Personal experience or data point]

[Reframe or takeaway]

[Engagement prompt: question or invitation]
```
**Length:** 100-200 words
**Frameworks:** Hormozi (Brevity = respect for reader's time), Cialdini (Social Proof via engagement)

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Gather Venture Configuration
Before producing content, collect or confirm:
- [ ] **Venture/Brand name** and founder name
- [ ] **ICP:** Who is the target reader? (role, net worth, geography, psychographics)
- [ ] **Tone split:** (e.g., 75% Founder-Honest / 25% Bold Challenger)
- [ ] **Language:** (e.g., German — formal Sie, English, bilingual)
- [ ] **Key differentiators:** (what makes this venture structurally different)
- [ ] **False beliefs to break:** (3 core misconceptions the audience holds)
- [ ] **Content home:** Where will this be published? (Substack, LinkedIn, blog, newsletter)
- [ ] **Existing content:** Any past articles, newsletters, or analyses to match voice

If this data isn't available, ask the user or check the venture folder for context files.

### Step 1b: Voice Training — Ingest the Founder's Existing Content
The skill can only write in the founder's voice if it has absorbed their existing content. Ask the founder to provide as many of these as possible:

**What to ingest:**
- **LinkedIn posts** — copy/paste their last 20-30 posts, or provide their LinkedIn profile URL for scraping
- **YouTube videos** — URLs to their videos/interviews/talks; extract transcripts via WebFetch
- **Substack/newsletter** — URLs to their archive; scrape the last 10-20 editions
- **Blog/website articles** — any published writing on their own domain
- **Podcast appearances** — episode URLs; extract transcripts
- **Internal writing** — investor letters, partner updates, memos (these reveal the most authentic voice)
- **Social media** — Twitter/X threads, Instagram captions with long-form text

**How to process:**
1. Fetch/read all provided content
2. Build a **Voice Profile** document that captures:
   - **Sentence structure:** Short and punchy? Long and analytical? Mixed?
   - **Opening patterns:** How do they typically start a piece? (question, story, statement, data?)
   - **Vocabulary fingerprint:** Words and phrases they use repeatedly (e.g., domain-specific terms, recurring metaphors, signature phrases unique to their writing)
   - **Emotional register:** Where on the spectrum from clinical → passionate → provocative?
   - **Metaphor style:** Do they use analogies? From which domains? (sports, war, cooking, building?)
   - **Formality level:** Contractions? Slang? Academic references?
   - **Signature moves:** Any recurring rhetorical devices? (e.g., "Here's the thing:", lists of three, rhetorical questions)
   - **What they NEVER do:** Identify anti-patterns (e.g., never uses emojis, never says "leverage" as a verb, never opens with "In today's world...")
3. Save the Voice Profile to: `{venture-folder}/content/voice-profile.md`
4. Reference this profile for every content piece — the reader should not be able to tell Claude wrote it

**Voice calibration check:** After writing the first draft, ask the founder: "Does this sound like you?" Iterate until they say yes, then update the Voice Profile with what you learned.

### Step 1c: Target Group Deep Research
Before creating content, the skill must deeply understand WHO the founder is writing for. This goes beyond demographics — it's about understanding the audience's real daily reality, fears, language, and failed attempts.

**When to run this step:**
- First time using the skill for a new venture (mandatory)
- When the founder identifies a new target segment
- When existing content isn't resonating (re-research)

**Ask the founder:** "Who specifically are you writing for? Describe your ideal reader."

Then execute a comprehensive research sweep:

#### Research Sources (scrape all of these)

**YouTube Transcript Mining:**
- `"{target group}" problems site:youtube.com`
- `"{target group}" mistakes interview`
- `"{target group}" day in the life`
- `"{target group}" frustrated` / `"{target group}" struggles`
- Conference talks, panel discussions, podcasts featuring the target group
- **Extract:** Direct quotes about frustrations, specific numbers they mention, emotional language, stories about failed solutions

**Forum & Community Mining:**
- Reddit: `"{target group}" biggest challenge site:reddit.com`
- Quora: questions the target group asks
- LinkedIn discussions and comments on relevant posts
- Facebook groups (public posts)
- Industry-specific forums (Verbände, trade associations, professional networks)
- **Extract:** Recurring complaints, questions they ask repeatedly, advice they give each other

**Industry Reports & Data:**
- Trade press / Fachpresse
- Industry association publications
- Government statistics (Statistisches Bundesamt, BFS, etc.)
- Consulting firm sector reports
- Academic research on the target group's operational challenges
- **Extract:** Hard numbers (%, EUR, hours) that quantify the pains

**Competitor Content Analysis:**
- What are competing voices in this space writing about?
- Which of their posts get the most engagement?
- What topics are oversaturated vs. underserved?
- **Extract:** Content gaps the founder can own

**News & Trends:**
- Recent regulatory changes affecting the target group
- Industry trend pieces showing growing pressure
- Macro shifts that create urgency

**Output: Target Group Foundations Document**
Save to `{venture-folder}/content/target-group-foundations.md`:
```markdown
# Target Group Foundations: {target group name}

## Demographics
- Role, geography, company size, net worth range

## Psychographic Profile
- How they think, what motivates them, what they value

## Top 5 Pains (ranked by severity, with sources)
1. {Pain} — "{direct quote}" (Source: {URL/platform})
   Cost: {specific EUR/hours/% number}

## What Keeps Them Up at Night
- {fears, anxieties, worst-case scenarios}

## What They've Tried (and Why It Failed)
- {Solution 1} — failed because {reason}

## Their Own Language
- Key phrases, jargon, terminology they use
- How they describe their problems (not how consultants describe them)

## Emotional Core
- Why they started, what frustrates them, what they dream of

## False Beliefs
1. Vehicle: {what they believe about solutions like yours}
2. Internal: {what they believe about their own ability}
3. External: {what they believe about external barriers}

## Content Gaps
- Topics competitors ignore or handle poorly
- Questions the audience asks that nobody answers well

## Sources
- {list of all URLs, forums, reports used}
```

This document becomes the foundation for ALL content. Every article should reference insights from it. Update it quarterly or when new research reveals shifts.

### Step 2: Accept Raw Input
The user provides one or more raw inputs:
- A partner/client question (verbatim or paraphrased)
- An investment analysis or thesis
- A market event or news item
- A contrarian observation or frustration
- A call transcript or meeting notes
- A competitor's claim to counter

For each input, identify:
1. Which **Content Type** (1-6) fits best
2. Which **frameworks** are most relevant
3. The **core insight** — the one thing the reader should walk away with

### Step 3: Generate Headlines (5 per piece)
For each content piece, produce 5 headline options:

**Headline Formulas (rotate through these):**
| Formula | Pattern |
|---------|---------|
| Question that names the pain | "Why do {X}% of {your prospects' peer group} {do the wrong thing}?" |
| Contrarian statement | "{Industry-accepted truth} is the most expensive lie in {your sector}" |
| Specific number + outcome | "The {EUR/USD X} mistake most {your ICP} make in year one" |
| "How [peer group] is..." | "How {your ICP} are {achieving outcome} without {expected vehicle}" |
| Quote from conversation | "'{Verbatim quote from a real prospect call that names the core pain}'" |

Replace each `{placeholder}` with content drawn from your Asset Foundations CSV. The patterns are framework-agnostic; the specifics must come from your customer transcripts.

**Rules:**
- Never clickbait — every headline must be deliverable in the article
- Use specific numbers over vague claims
- Name the audience in the headline when possible
- Test emotional resonance: would the ICP stop scrolling for this?

### Step 4: Write the Draft
Using the selected Content Type template:

1. **Open with the reader's reality** — not the founder's credentials. DK: Enter the conversation in their head.
2. **Agitate before solving** — make the cost of the status quo vivid. Use specific EUR amounts, time periods, percentages.
3. **Tell a story** — Brunson's Epiphany Bridge. Every piece needs a moment where the reader goes "oh, THAT's why."
4. **Give away the thinking** — Hormozi: share the framework, not just the conclusion. The reader should learn something real.
5. **Break one false belief** — explicitly address one misconception the audience holds.
6. **End with implicit authority** — Cialdini: the reader should finish thinking "this person really knows this space" without being told so.
7. **Soft CTA** — invite engagement, never hard-sell. "If you're navigating this too, I'd be curious to hear your approach."

**Writing Rules:**
- Write in the founder's voice (match their existing tone from past content)
- Use the audience's own jargon and terminology
- Specific > generic. Always. "{specific number} over {specific time period}" > "a lot of money over time"
- One core idea per piece — if it branches, split into two pieces
- No filler paragraphs. Every paragraph must advance the argument or deepen the story.
- Mark any claims that need the founder's verification with `[VERIFY: ...]`
- Mark any placeholder data with `[DATA: ...]`

### Step 7: Platform Optimization
Before finalizing, adapt the content for the target platform. Each platform has different rules for what performs best. The founder specifies where this piece will be published, and the skill formats accordingly.

#### Substack / Newsletter
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Headline** | 6-12 words, curiosity-driven or contrarian. Avoid clickbait — Substack readers punish it. |
| **Subtitle** | 1 sentence that complements (not repeats) the headline. Shows up in email preview. |
| **Length** | 800-2,000 words (sweet spot: 1,200). Readers expect depth — this isn't social media. |
| **Images** | 1-3 max. One header image (optional), 1-2 inline if they add meaning (charts, diagrams). No stock photos. |
| **Formatting** | Use H2/H3 subheadings every 200-300 words. Bold key phrases. Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences). Pull quotes for emphasis. |
| **CTA** | End with a question or invitation to reply. "Hit reply and tell me..." works well — Substack rewards engagement. |
| **Frequency** | 1-2x/week for growth. Consistency > frequency. Same day/time each week. |
| **Email preview** | First 2-3 lines appear in inbox — make them count. No "Hi everyone" or throat-clearing. |

#### LinkedIn
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Hook** | First 2 lines are everything — they appear before "...see more". Must create curiosity or tension. |
| **Length** | 150-300 words for posts. 800-1,300 characters performs best. LinkedIn truncates at ~210 characters before fold. |
| **Format** | Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each). Line breaks between every paragraph. Use → or • for lists, not markdown bullets. |
| **Images** | Single image posts get 2x engagement vs. text-only. Carousels (PDF uploads) get 3x. No links in post body (kills reach). |
| **Links** | Put links in the FIRST COMMENT, not the post. LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links. |
| **Hashtags** | 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end. Don't overdo it. |
| **CTA** | End with a question. "What's your take?" / "Agree or disagree?" / "Share your experience below." Engagement = distribution. |
| **Best times** | Tue-Thu, 7-8am or 12-1pm local time of your ICP. |
| **Articles vs. Posts** | Posts get 5-10x the reach of LinkedIn Articles. Use posts for distribution, link to Substack for depth. |

#### Instagram
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Format types** | Carousel (10 slides) > Reel (60-90s) > Single image > Story. Carousels get highest saves and shares. |
| **Carousel slides** | Slide 1: bold hook statement (treat like a headline). Slides 2-9: one insight per slide, large text, minimal design. Slide 10: CTA + profile tag. |
| **Caption** | Up to 2,200 characters, but first 125 show before "...more". Front-load the hook. |
| **Reel length** | 60-90 seconds for educational content. Hook in first 3 seconds. Subtitles mandatory (85% watch on mute). |
| **Hashtags** | 5-15 in caption or first comment. Mix broad (#investing) with niche (#wealthmanagement #postexitlife). |
| **Aesthetic** | Consistent visual brand. Define a primary + accent (e.g., a navy + an orange) and apply consistently across all content. Clean, not cluttered. |
| **Stories** | Behind-the-scenes, polls, Q&A, quick takes. Less polished = more authentic. Use for engagement, not reach. |

#### TikTok
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Length** | 60-180 seconds for educational/finance content. Shorter for hooks, longer for depth. |
| **Hook** | First 1-3 seconds MUST stop the scroll. "Here's what nobody tells you about..." / "This is the biggest lie in [industry]..." |
| **Format** | Talking head (face to camera) performs best for authority content. Green screen with charts/data as B-roll. |
| **Subtitles** | Mandatory. Large, readable, high-contrast. TikTok's auto-captions are unreliable — add manually. |
| **Hashtags** | 3-5. Include one broad (#finance, #investing) and niche (#wealthmanagement, #entrepreneurlife). |
| **Sound** | Trending sounds boost discovery but may not fit professional content. Original audio is fine for talking-head. |
| **CTA** | "Follow for more" + engagement question in caption. Pin a comment with the key takeaway. |
| **Posting** | 1-3x/day for growth. Consistency matters more than production quality. Raw > polished on TikTok. |

#### Medium
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Headline** | Clear, specific, SEO-friendly. Medium ranks on Google — optimize for search intent. |
| **Length** | 1,000-2,500 words (7-minute read is the sweet spot based on Medium's own data). |
| **Images** | 1 header image (1500×1000px recommended). Inline images for charts/data. No decorative stock photos. |
| **Formatting** | H2 subheadings every 300 words. Bold key phrases. Block quotes for emphasis. Code blocks for data/formulas. |
| **Tags** | 5 tags max. Choose high-traffic tags relevant to your topic. |
| **Publications** | Submit to relevant publications (e.g., The Startup, Better Marketing, DataDrivenInvestor) for 10-100x reach. |
| **SEO** | Include target keywords in title, subtitle, first paragraph, and at least one H2. |

#### Personal Website / Blog
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| **Length** | 1,000-3,000 words. Longer evergreen pieces for SEO, shorter for commentary. |
| **SEO** | Title tag (60 chars), meta description (155 chars), H2/H3 structure, internal links to other articles. |
| **Images** | Header image + 1-2 inline. Optimize for web (compress, alt text, lazy loading). |
| **CTA** | Email capture (newsletter signup) on every article. Sticky or end-of-article placement. |
| **Schema** | Add Article schema markup for rich snippets in Google. |
| **Cross-posting** | Publish on website FIRST (canonical URL), then cross-post to Medium/LinkedIn 2-3 days later with canonical link. |

#### Platform-Specific Repurposing Flow
One piece of content should be adapted across platforms, not copy-pasted:
```
Long-form article (Substack/Blog, 1,200 words)
  → LinkedIn post (300 words, key insight only, link in comment)
  → Instagram carousel (10 slides, 1 insight per slide)
  → TikTok/Reel script (90 seconds, hook + 1 story + 1 takeaway)
  → Twitter/X thread (5-8 tweets, numbered, hook in tweet 1)
  → LinkedIn newsletter edition (if different from Substack audience)
```

When producing content, always specify which platform(s) the piece targets and apply the relevant formatting rules above. If the founder wants multi-platform distribution, produce the long-form version first, then the adapted versions.

### Step 8: Output & Delivery
Content can be delivered in multiple ways depending on the founder's workflow:

#### Option A: Local Markdown Files (default)
- Save drafts to: `{venture-folder}/content/{YYYY-MM-slug}.md`
- Push to venture's GitHub repo if configured

#### Option B: Google Docs (for iterative review)
Create a Google Doc for each content piece so the founder can review, comment, and request iterations directly:
1. Create the doc via Google Docs API (using the venture's OAuth credentials or shared access)
2. Insert the full draft with proper heading styles (H1 title, H2 sections, bold key phrases)
3. Move to the venture's shared Google Drive folder
4. Share the doc URL with the founder
5. The founder reviews, leaves comments or edits, then asks Claude to iterate
6. Claude reads the doc, processes feedback, and updates the doc with the next version
7. Repeat until the founder approves → status changes from DRAFT to READY

**Naming convention:** `{Venture} Content — {YYYY-MM-DD} — {headline slug}`
**Folder structure:** `{Shared Drive Folder}/Content Drafts/`

#### Option C: Notion Pages (for teams using Notion)
Create a Notion page for each content piece:
1. Create a page in the founder's Notion workspace (via Notion API/MCP)
2. Use Notion's native blocks: heading blocks for sections, callout blocks for key insights, toggle blocks for metadata
3. Add a "Status" property: Draft → In Review → Approved → Published
4. Add properties: Type, Platform, Publish Date, Headline Options
5. The founder reviews in Notion, leaves comments, toggles status
6. Claude reads the page, processes feedback, updates with next iteration

**Database structure:** Create a "Content Pipeline" database with views:
- **Kanban view:** by Status (Draft / In Review / Approved / Published)
- **Calendar view:** by Publish Date
- **Table view:** all pieces with Type, Platform, Status, Headline

#### Iterative Review Workflow (applies to all output options)
The skill supports iterative refinement:
1. **First draft** → deliver to founder with headline options and `[VERIFY]`/`[DATA]` markers
2. **Founder feedback** → "make it more direct", "this section is weak", "wrong tone here", "add the anecdote about X"
3. **Revision** → Claude applies feedback, updates the doc/page/file, highlights what changed
4. **Repeat** → until founder says "approved" or "ready to publish"
5. **Final step** → apply Platform Optimization formatting for the target platform(s)

Present all headline options for founder selection. Flag any `[VERIFY]` or `[DATA]` markers for founder review.

## Quality Checklist
Before delivering any content piece, verify:
- [ ] Opens with the **reader's pain or reality**, not the founder's bio
- [ ] At least one **specific number** (EUR, %, years, hours) in the first 3 paragraphs
- [ ] **Epiphany Bridge** moment present — a clear "aha" turning point
- [ ] **One false belief** explicitly addressed and broken
- [ ] **Social proof** woven in (peer conversations, anonymized examples, industry data)
- [ ] **No hard sell** — CTA is an invitation, not a pitch
- [ ] Written in the **founder's authentic voice** (match tone split)
- [ ] Uses the **audience's own jargon** (not generic business language)
- [ ] Every claim is either sourced or marked `[VERIFY]`
- [ ] **Headline** passes the "would they stop scrolling?" test
- [ ] Piece can **stand alone** — no required context from other articles
- [ ] **Value delivered** — reader learns something real, even if they never become a client (Hormozi: give away the secrets)
- [ ] No unsupported superlatives ("best", "world-class", "unrivaled") — show, don't tell
- [ ] **Cialdini check:** at least 3 of 7 principles are active in the piece
- [ ] **Voice match:** Draft sounds like the founder, not like an AI (reference Voice Profile)
- [ ] **Target group research** informed the pain points and language used
- [ ] **Platform formatting** applied — length, structure, CTA, and media match the target platform's best practices

### How to fill it in
- **`differentiators`** must be hard-edged. Soft claims ("we care more") are not differentiators. Anything a competitor could say tomorrow is not a differentiator.
- **`false_beliefs`** are the three counter-claims your content will systematically dismantle. Vehicle = the alternative they think is better. Internal = the story they tell themselves about why they can't act. External = the story they tell themselves about the market or industry.
- **`tone_split`** should match your founder's natural register. If they're not analytical-contrarian by default, do not force it.

### Raw Input Examples (replace with your own)
- **Customer question:** Pull a real question your customers have asked recently. Use it as the spine of an article.
- **Internal analysis:** Take a deep-dive your team has already produced for an internal audience. Translate to public.
- **Market event:** A macro or industry shift that affects your prospects this week.
- **Contrarian observation:** A specific data point that contradicts industry conventional wisdom — sourced and citable.

## Adaptation Notes
This skill is venture-agnostic. When applying:
- Fill in the placeholders above using your Asset Foundations as the source.
- Adjust content types to match your founder's natural output — a SaaS founder might do more Type 2 (how-to analysis) and less Type 4 (market commentary).
- Match the language and formality to your audience (Sie/Du for German, formal/casual for English).
- For B2B SaaS: emphasize ROI calculations, integration stories, before/after workflows.
- For services: emphasize relationships, trust signals, transformation narratives.
- For consumer: emphasize identity, community, aspiration.
